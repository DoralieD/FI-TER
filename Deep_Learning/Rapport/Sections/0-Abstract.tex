\begin{abstract}
The scarcity of accessible Electronic Health Records (EHR) and continuous patient monitoring data, heavily restricted by privacy regulations, remains a significant bottleneck for applying Deep Learning in healthcare. To address this data drought, this paper presents a robust, rule-based synthetic data generation pipeline designed to simulate a realistic Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) environment. We successfully generated a vast multimodal cohort of 200,000 synthetic patient records, encompassing continuous vital signs, static demographic history, and discrete laboratory biomarkers across 24 distinct medical pathologies. Furthermore, to validate the complexity and clinical coherence of our synthetic dataset, we benchmarked two Deep Learning architectures: an Early Fusion Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) and an advanced Contextual Fusion Tabular Transformer. Our experimental results prove that the synthetic data presents a challenging, non-trivial classification task, with the models achieving high predictive performance (Accuracy, F1-Score, ROC-AUC) only when effectively fusing the multimodal features. Finally, the integration of SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) confirms that the neural networks learned genuine physiological rules programmed into the generation engine, validating our synthetic dataset as a trustworthy proxy for real-world IoMT predictive modeling.
\end{abstract}
